WNLR Scholarship Program

The Wednesday Night Laundry Runners have instituted a scholarship program to reward outstanding high school distance runners. The purpose of the program is to recognize local excellence in athletic and academic achievement, commitment to community and to help our Monterey County student-athletes achieve broader success. Nominated by their coaches, these awards help ease some of the financial burdens of college.

The WNLR Scholarship Program, founded by Mike Dove in 2001, started modestly with a few thousand dollars in the first few years. Today, the annual fund raises anywhere from $8,000 - $12,000 and has awarded a total of $143,000 to 136 students since its inception.

Donations to the scholarship fund are from individual WNLR members, private donations, and a generous donation from the Big Sur Marathon Foundation.

Qualifications for each nominee are the following:

All student runners must be nominated by their high school coaches.

  • Must be a Monterey County high school senior distance runner
  • Must compete in BOTH cross country and distance track and field (800,1600, and/or 3200)
  • The student must plan to compete in a two or four-year college. (exceptions may apply)
  • Loves running
  • Team leader and role model
  • Successful high school runner in competition
  • High academic achievement (Minimum GPA is 3.0)
  • Participate in extracurricular activities
  • Family monetary need is considered
